Why It Matters

Health Benefits of True Cinnamon

Ceylon cinnamon is the only "true" cinnamon — its low coumarin content (under 0.004%) makes it daily-safe, unlike cassia. Centuries of Ayurvedic tradition and modern research point to remarkable benefits.

Blood Sugar Support Studies suggest Ceylon cinnamon may help moderate post-meal glucose response.
Powerful Antioxidants Rich in polyphenols — among the highest of any common kitchen spice.
Anti-inflammatory Cinnamaldehyde compounds traditionally used to soothe inflammation.
Heart-Healthy Daily-safe coumarin levels — unlike cassia, which limits intake.
In the Kitchen

Our Favourite Uses

  • Steep whole: Drop a stick into mulled wine, chai, or apple cider — it releases slowly.
  • Infuse milk: Simmer in warm milk with cardamom and a touch of honey for an evening ritual.
  • Roast meats: Lamb or duck with whole sticks, star anise, and cloves.
  • Grind fresh: Snap and grind in a coffee mill for the freshest dust on baked apples or oats.
  • Cold brew: Add a stick to a cold-brew coffee jar for 12 hours — magic.
Origin

From the Wijeratne Family Garden

These quills are hand-peeled by the Wijeratne family — a fourth-generation cinnamon-growing household in Matale. Cinnamon-peeling is a craft passed down by feel, not measurement: the bark must be lifted at the precise moment when the inner cambium is ready to release.

The peeled bark is rolled by hand into thin quills, then sun-dried for five to seven days on woven palm mats. The entire process — from tree to tin — takes under three weeks.
